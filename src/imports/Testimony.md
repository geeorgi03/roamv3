## 1MILLION Dance Studio Cofounder Lia Kim Shares the Secrets of Her Success

*Spotify for Artists — November 9, 2022*

As the cofounder of 1MILLION Dance Studio, Lia Kim is one of the most influential choreographers in the world. Her routines have been foundational to the studio's success, which continues to attract top dancers and collaborate with leading brands and artists.

The studio has also built a worldwide fan base through YouTube, and earlier in 2022 launched an official Spotify profile featuring tracks from popular videos and curated playlists from top instructors.

Lia has danced professionally since age 12 and has personally choreographed for BoA, Girls' Generation, and SUNMI. Building community and making dance accessible for everyone is what she values most.

---

### Interview Highlights

**Which Spotify feature interests you the most?**

The social listening feature. Listeners sharing music tastes through social media, and communicating in real time, aligns with 1MILLION's mission.

**What is the new dance trend, and how is 1MILLION adopting it?**

Right now there's a range of trends — retro songs from the '80s, '90s, even '50s and '60s are trendier than new releases. We've been choreographing to older songs a lot lately.

**What differentiates 1MILLION Dance Studio from others?**

We are not only a space for top dancers but also a space to popularize and globalize dance. 70% of students are from other countries. We've visited over 20 countries and dance with about 500 people per workshop. It's amazing to see people of different cultures and languages communicate through dance.

**If you were to pick a music genre that embodies 1MILLION's identity?**

Definitely hip-hop! Hip-hop sets new trends.

**What is your ultimate goal?**

We want to enrich lives through dance. I hope 1MILLION becomes a place where people can come freely to dance, the way they'd go out for a drink after work. There's no such thing as a bad dancer.

**Advice for those who want to dance but are afraid?**

Don't be shy; just dance! Listen to your heart and body, and go for it.

**What is special about K-Dance?**

Perfect synchronization. There's no other country that can outperform Korea in skill, technique, quality, or training. Not a single mistake — that's what makes K-Dance special.

**Criteria for selecting songs?**

Good beats, vocals, and melodies. Artists like Bruno Mars and The Weeknd.

**Your philosophy on dance?**

Dance is all about confidence. It's not difficult — feel the rhythm, immerse yourself in the song, and move however you want.

**Where do you get inspiration?**

I watch various videos including movies, study other choreographers, and even watch 1MILLION's beginner-level students for new ideas. I listen to songs repeatedly to understand the songwriter's message and the artist's habits. When everything integrates into the choreography, it feels more natural.

## [Exclusive Interview] Choreographer Jay Kim — Teaching Dance in 50 Countries

*SOKOLLAB — November 22, 2023*

Jay Kim has choreographed and danced with stars including Luhan (EXO), Taemin (SHINee), and Jay Park. Known for work at 1Million Dance Studio and Loen Entertainment Dance Studio, he has taught dance across 100 cities in 50 countries.

---

### Interview Highlights

**How did you get started in dance?**

I started b-boying/breakdancing at 14, but was more interested in working with movement to create something. I preferred choreography because it gave me independence and freedom to express myself.

**How has teaching shaped your approach to choreography?**

I pay close attention to expression so students feel the emotions I want to emphasize — when you dance for a long time, movements and emotions can become artificial.

**How do you adapt when working with students of different styles?**

Style isn't a big issue. What matters is whether you have your *own* style. Dancers with their own style can perform any genre in their own way. The basics are the important parts of all dances.

**Where do you find inspiration?**

When I spend time with family and friends. I create choreography based on the mood, emotions, and memories of those moments.

**Favourite countries visited?**

South Africa, Hungary, Switzerland, Canada, Colombia, and Brazil. Would love to host a workshop in New York.

**Choreographers you admire?**

I respect anyone who has walked this path for many years. Given the chance, I'd love to collaborate with Kyle Hanagami from LA.

**Most memorable moment?**

When everyone hugged me after my dance at a workshop in Mexico — that's when I found my own reason for dancing.

**Advice for aspiring dancers?**

Be yourself. Each of us has our own talents. Don't be impatient — consider what you can contribute to the world and follow your own path.

## STEEZY — How to Choreograph a Dance Routine (Clay / ClayDohBoon)

*Transcript excerpt*

Clay, Director of Video at STEEZY and professional dancer/choreographer, walks through his choreography process live.

**Music selection:** Uses Spotify, SoundCloud, or Hype M to find music. Picks a song by a friend — *"Tell Nobody"* by Nieman, noting strong hi-hats, pitch variation in the vocals.

**Process highlights:**

- Finds the exact section of music he wants to work with (hi-hats, bass accents)
- First move is the hardest — reverts to basics (waves, broken waves) when stuck
- Freestyles when blocked, then refines
- Finds visual inspiration in unexpected places (first letter of the artist's name → arm shape)
- Writes down beats and counts when needed: *"It's okay to just sit down and figure it out"*
- Concepts emerge organically — a "two people" theme, a Jackie Chan drunken master feel
- Directionality awareness: notices he keeps facing right, intentionally ends left
- Revises constantly — removes moves that don't work, keeps what does

> *"Overall, choreographing is not an easy thing to do. Just because you can't finish a choreography in 1 hour, 2 weeks, a month — doesn't mean you should stop."*

## STEEZY — How to Choreograph (Jun Quemado)

*Transcript excerpt*

Jun, STEEZY instructor, choreographs to *"Temporary"* by Brian Puspos live on camera.

**Process highlights:**

- Very precise, movement-based on lyrics: writes lyrics out first, plans in two 8-counts at a time with space for detail notes
- Builds in small sets, plays music back, then reworks — doesn't push straight through
- Avoids repeating the same move or concept more than once in a piece
- When there's no lyrical space to dance in, throws in a transitional move as prep for the next
- Concept-driven: waking up → stretching → falling in love → drunken master → sure shot/bow-and-arrow
- Calls the songwriter directly to verify a lyric ("Got me in a love lock")
- Collaborates in the moment — Clay suggests loosening up for the "drunken stumble" concept, which Jun incorporates
- Connects moves so nothing feels isolated; leaves ending open — does a crazy concept before, then lets it die down

> *"It's very good to sometimes have an additional person to see what you want. Two different concepts kind of put everything together."*
